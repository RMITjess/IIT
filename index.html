Project Idea

Overview

My project idea is an app that will provide Australian adults with clear and concise advice on what path to take to either upskill in their current job role or completely change career paths and/or learn new skills with TAFE and Uni opportunities. It will require the use of a lot of personal and sensitive information and this may require a good strategy so that users can trust in the product that their information is protected. A development team with various skills will be needed for the app to be deployed and successful.

Motivation

According to the ING Future Focus Report released late May 2020, 3.3 million Australian adults are rethinking their career paths in light of the COVID-19 pandemic. The report highlighted that 32% of people would find work opportunities hard to come by and 28% learning new skills in isolation to either progress in their careers or change their career path entirely. I spoke to a few colleagues and thought of my own experiences when signing up to online courses or upskilling in my current position and there was a general feeling of confusion, waste of money and frustration of how we get to where we want to be. This made me think of developing an app that would incorporate various online tools to gather data in one place to provide an outcome of which direction to go in, what skills are needed and whether to begin education in TAFE or Uni.

Description

The project should be a simple data sharing app using an algorithm that aggregates useful information on an individual’s person’s identity, interests, attitude, current skills, ambitions and then provides an outcome of the path that individual should take in their career or study, whether it be further study or a simple course at TAFE that suits their current lifestyle, financial situation and skillset.

The app will use information from various sources that the user will either have a profile with or has interacted with before. Custom applications and API’s such as Myers Briggs personality test, study records, current employment information including job description, social media accounts, skills obtained, seek.com.au, TAFE and Uni courses available; will need to be used to connect all the components and then crawl all the information to create the outcome for the end-user that will enable them to make confident and cost-efficient choices on their careers.

The app will be easy to navigate with usability at the forefront of design as the user experience will determine the success of the app. If doesn’t have simplicity, then users will not want to use it and so the below features are an important aspect of how this app will need to run.

  • Easy navigation with usability at the forefront of the design.
  • Easy sign up process with social integration, allowing users to stay engaged and also share information across various platforms.
  • Security will be an important feature of the app as there will be personal and sensitive information about the user.
  • Geolocation integration will provide the user with directions or nearby institutions/office buildings that
  • Run multiple tests and QA so that the app is always reliable
  • Ability for users to provide feedback so that the development team can troubleshoot accordingly.
  • Push Notifications so that users can see if there is a Job, Study Course or any other opportunity available in their area.

Outcome

If this project was successful, then we will be able to solve many issues Australians face when deciding what they want to do with their life. These issues can be thousands of dollars in study debt, avoiding mental health problems by accepting a job that doesn’t really fit with your values or skillset and feeling stuck there as you gear it will not look good on your resume to leave. The intention of the app is to give users a more positive outlook on career and study opportunities and reduce anxiety about whether change is possible or not, no matter what stage or age you are at.
